The Telangana State Prisons Department is functioning under the control of Home Department. The Director General and Correctional Services is the Head of the Department. He is assisted by 1 Inspector General of Prisons at Headquarters and 2 Deputy Inspectors General of Prisons at Range Level. The various institutions under the control of the Prisons Department are classified into Central Prisons, District Jails, Open Prisons, Special Prison for Women and Sub Jails. They are headed by officers of different ranks.

The Director General of Prisons & Correctional Services is the Controlling Officer of the Prisons Department. To assist the Director General of Prisons at the directorate, there is 1 Inspector General of Prisons looking after the subjects of Headquarters. Other officers working in Headquarters include Administrative Officer to Director General of Prisons,Asst. Accounts Officer

The Department is divided into two Ranges i.e Hyderabad Range and Warangal Ranges, each headed by Deputy Inspector General of Prisons. The headquarters of the two regions are located at Hyderabad and Warangal respectively.

The Hyderabad Range consists of (3) districts with largest number of institutions. About 50% of prison population usually been confined in the jails located in this region, while the Warangal Region have jurisdiction of 4 Districts

The efficiency of an organization is determined by the caliber of training and motivation of the personnel manning it. This is particularly so in respect of organization that deals with human beings. Therefore, very special care and attention should be bestowed in selection and training of personnel who are to man the prison and Correctional Administration. Physical fitness and courage, leadership and man management qualities as well as balanced humane personality are essentially required for prison personnel.

Directors Message

Vinoy Kumar Singh, a 1987 batch AP Cadre IPS officer is a legendary police officer. His professional integrity, social policing, boldness to call 'Spade a Spade' and sterling performance against the Naxalites and the criminal warlords in Andhra Pradesh and Bihar is simply unparalleled. He hails from Akhgaon village of Bhojpur District of Bihar and worked both in Andhra Pradesh and Bihar Police in different capacities.

At the behest of Bihar Police, he raised 'Special Task Force' in Bihar and achieved unexpected result against dreaded ganglords. He became a household name as a police officer and many editorials and TV reels were run on him. Aaj Tak, the Hindi news channel ran a 40 - minute documentary on him for his exploits in Bihar.

He introduced many innovative methods like people's council, social welfare and development cell, emergency card etc under the aegis of police that changed the rules of game and threatened the very existence of vested interests.

The concept of "Police as a social worker" was taken up so seriously that Government found him very inconvenient. He is a hardcore policeman and has foreseen scope and vast possibilities of socio - economic changes through police efforts. His passion is policing and mission ' changes in police '. His vision of policing is boundless, holistic, moral and all comprehensive. His philosophy is : Police can change the society but everywhere they are fettered, manoeuvered and misused through their own subculture, officers and men and powers-that-be. In nutshell, he is less a writer and more an explorer, experimenter, visionary and path-breaker.
