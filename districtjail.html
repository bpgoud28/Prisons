As per the PWD records, the Heritage jail was constructed in the year 1796 A.D., during the Prime ministership of Salar Jung I. This prison is spread across an area of three acres and 10 guntas out of which jail building occupies one acre and 12 guntas. At present, this Heritage jail building consists of two wings i.e., male and female wing with nine barracks in male wing and one barrack in female wing. In over two centuries of its history, the Sangareddy jail was under the supervision of heads of various departments such as revenue, judiciary and medical departments. Till 1981, this jail was under the control of medical department. In 1981, the responsibility of District Jail, Sangareddy was shifted to the Prisons Department and was headed by a Jail superintendent in the cadre of Deputy Superintendent of Jails. This old District Jail is now preserved as Heritage structure and converted into a Museum to house artefacts, equipments showing the history of Jails.The new building is at Kandhi village, functioning from 5-5-2012.

The District Jail, Nalgonda was constructed in 1916 during the Nizam's era. it was a District Jail in Hyderabad State. After formation of Andhra Pradesh it was downgraded as Sub Jail Grade II, Later the Post of Superintendent in the cadre of Deputy Superintendent of Jails was created in the year 1980. This Jail has been in the control of Jail's Department since 05-01-1981.

Total area of land 15 Acers 13 guntas Total Capacity 130 Male + 07 Female = 137 Residential Quarters 16 Store Room 03 Female Ward 01

The old District Jail locally called as Khilla Jail was a Fort located on a Hillock of about 300 feet height. It was a part of Sri Raghunath Mandir which was constructed during the period of Chatrapathi Shivaji, in time the Temple chowltrees were converted into Jail for confining political activities who were acting against the Nizam’s regime. It was upgraded as Central Prison for a short period in 1969 and it was again downgraded as District Jail

This District Jail was constructed during the Nizam period in the year 1916 as Sub Jail. Previously the District Head Quarters was situated at Asifabad. Later it was shifted to Adilabad which is 120 kms away from Asifabad. This jail was closed during the year 1951 after jail break. The prison was re-opened as a District Jail after (40) years during the year 1991 i.e., on 15.03.1991. Three (03) new barracks were constructed during the year 2001 for additional accommodation of the prisoners. The present authorize accommodation is 223 prisoners including female Barrack.

Directors Message

Vinoy Kumar Singh, a 1987 batch AP Cadre IPS officer is a legendary police officer. His professional integrity, social policing, boldness to call 'Spade a Spade' and sterling performance against the Naxalites and the criminal warlords in Andhra Pradesh and Bihar is simply unparalleled. He hails from Akhgaon village of Bhojpur District of Bihar and worked both in Andhra Pradesh and Bihar Police in different capacities.

At the behest of Bihar Police, he raised 'Special Task Force' in Bihar and achieved unexpected result against dreaded ganglords. He became a household name as a police officer and many editorials and TV reels were run on him. Aaj Tak, the Hindi news channel ran a 40 - minute documentary on him for his exploits in Bihar.

He introduced many innovative methods like people's council, social welfare and development cell, emergency card etc under the aegis of police that changed the rules of game and threatened the very existence of vested interests.

The concept of "Police as a social worker" was taken up so seriously that Government found him very inconvenient. He is a hardcore policeman and has foreseen scope and vast possibilities of socio - economic changes through police efforts. His passion is policing and mission ' changes in police '. His vision of policing is boundless, holistic, moral and all comprehensive. His philosophy is : Police can change the society but everywhere they are fettered, manoeuvered and misused through their own subculture, officers and men and powers-that-be. In nutshell, he is less a writer and more an explorer, experimenter, visionary and path-breaker.
